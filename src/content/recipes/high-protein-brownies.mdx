  - "Preheat the oven to 350°F / 180°C. Line a small baking tray or pan with parchment paper."
  - "In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, chocolate protein powder, sweetener, and salt."
  - "Add the egg whites, eggs, cottage cheese, applesauce, maple syrup, and vanilla to a blender. Blend for about 10 seconds, just until smooth."
  - "Pour the wet mixture into the dry ingredients and fold until a thick brownie batter forms."
  - "Spread the batter into the lined pan. Add chocolate chips on top if using."
  - "Bake for 12 to 15 minutes, depending on how fudgy you want the center."
  - "Cool completely before slicing. For the best texture, chill the brownies in the fridge before serving."

These high-protein brownies are the kind of recipe that make “healthy dessert” feel a lot less tragic. They’re rich, chocolatey, easy to throw together, and the macros are strong enough to earn some respect.

## Why You’ll Love This Recipe

These brownies are quick, simple, and high in protein without turning into dry little bricks. They work well as a post-workout snack, a low-effort meal prep dessert, or a way to satisfy a chocolate craving without detonating your day.

## Simple Ingredients

The ingredient list is short and practical. Cottage cheese keeps the texture moist, chocolate protein powder boosts the protein, and applesauce plus maple syrup add just enough sweetness to keep things balanced.
