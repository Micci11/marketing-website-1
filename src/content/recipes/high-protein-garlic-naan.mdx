ingredientsDry:
  - "60g (2.1 oz) faba bean flour"
  - "60g (2.1 oz) self-raising flour"
  - "1 tsp onion powder"

ingredientsWet:
  - "100g (3.5 oz) 0% fat Greek yoghurt"

optionalIngredients:
  - "100g (3.5 oz) light mozzarella, divided"
  - "8g (1½ tsp) light butter, melted"
  - "8g (1 tsp) minced garlic"
  - "Fresh parsley, chopped"

instructions:
  - "In a bowl, combine the faba bean flour, self-raising flour, Greek yoghurt, and onion powder."
  - "Mix until a soft dough forms."
  - "Divide the dough into 2 equal portions."
  - "Roll each portion out on a lightly floured surface."
  - "Add half of the mozzarella to each piece."
  - "Fold the dough over the cheese and seal the edges."
  - "Gently roll out again into a naan shape."
  - "Heat a pan over medium heat."
  - "Cook each naan for 2–3 minutes per side until golden and cooked through."
  - "Mix the melted butter with minced garlic and parsley."
  - "Brush over the warm naan before serving."

tips:
  - "Lightly flour your surface to prevent sticking when rolling."
  - "Seal the edges well so the cheese stays inside while cooking."
  - "Cook on medium heat to avoid burning before the inside cooks through."

variations:
  - "Use regular flour instead of faba bean flour if needed (protein will be lower)."
  - "Add chili flakes or garlic powder to the dough for extra flavor."
  - "Skip the cheese filling for a lighter version."

storage:
  - "Store in the fridge for up to 2 days."
  - "Reheat in a pan or air fryer for best texture."

## Why You’ll Love This Recipe

This naan is soft, cheesy, and packed with protein while still tasting like real comfort food. The garlic butter finish takes it to another level and makes it feel way more indulgent than it actually is.

## Simple Ingredients

A mix of faba bean flour and self-raising flour creates a higher-protein dough, while Greek yoghurt keeps it soft and easy to work with. The mozzarella adds a melty center and the garlic butter brings everything together.

## Best Ways to Enjoy It

Serve it warm straight from the pan for the best texture. It pairs perfectly with curries, grilled meats, or even on its own as a high-protein snack.
