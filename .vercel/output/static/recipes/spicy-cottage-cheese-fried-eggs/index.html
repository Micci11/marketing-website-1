Spicy Cottage Cheese Fried Eggs

Creamy, spicy fried eggs cooked with cottage cheese for a high-protein twist. Rich, flavorful, and ready in minutes.

These eggs are creamy, slightly crispy on the edges, and packed with flavor. The cottage cheese adds extra protein and richness while keeping it balanced.

Serve straight from the pan with toast or wrap it in a tortilla. It’s perfect for a quick high-protein breakfast or an easy savory snack.

Spicy Cottage Cheese Fried Eggs

Prep Time

5 mins

Cook Time

5 mins

Total Time

10 mins

Servings

1

Calories

300

Protein

26g

Carbs

5g

Fat

20g

Ingredients

Switch between US and metric measurements. Metric conversions are approximate.

Dry Ingredients

  • ½ tsp seasoning (or salt and black pepper, to taste) 3ml seasoning (or salt and black pepper, to taste)

Wet Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs 2 large eggs
  • ½ cup (120g) cottage cheese 120ml (120g) cottage cheese
  • ½ tbsp chili oil (plus extra for topping, optional) 8ml chili oil (plus extra for topping, optional)

Optional

  • ½ small shallot, finely minced ½ small shallot, finely minced
  • Fresh chives, chopped Fresh chives, chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat a skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the cottage cheese and chili oil to the pan.
  3. Cook for 1–2 minutes until the cottage cheese softens and becomes creamy.
  4. Crack the eggs into a bowl or directly into the pan, keeping the yolks intact.
  5. Add the seasoning and minced shallots.
  6. Cover the pan and cook for 2–3 minutes until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still soft.
  7. Remove from heat and garnish with chives and optional extra chili oil.
  8. Serve immediately.

Tips

  • Let the cottage cheese melt slightly before adding the eggs for the best texture.
  • Covering the pan helps cook the tops of the eggs evenly.
  • Keep the heat medium to avoid burning the dairy.

Variations

  • Use heavy cream instead of cottage cheese for a richer version.
  • Add spinach or mushrooms for extra volume.
  • Top with parmesan or feta for extra flavor.

Storage

  • Best eaten fresh. Not ideal for storing as the texture changes.

FAQ

Does it taste like cottage cheese?

Not really. Once heated, it becomes creamy and blends into the eggs.

Can I make this less spicy?

Yes. Reduce or skip the chili oil.

Can I use egg whites?

Yes, but the dish will be less rich and creamy.
